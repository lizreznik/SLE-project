Recognized by The New York Times as “a trumpeter of versatility and poise”, Tatum Greenblatt is highly regarded as one of New York City’s most in-demand trumpet players across a broad range of styles and genres. A Bach Brass Artist and faculty member at The Juilliard School, Tatum appears frequently with The Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra in performances at Rose Hall, touring domestically, in recordings on The 50’s: a Prism, Big Band Holidays Vol 2, Ted Nash and Glenn Close’s Transformation, and as a featured Guest Soloist for Wynton Marsalis’ 60th Birthday Celebration. In addition to his performance career, Tatum is a dedicated educator. He has served on faculty in the Jazz Department at Juilliard since 2017, spent five years on faculty at Rutgers University, and performs numerous roles within the Education Department at Jazz at Lincoln Center, including as Director of the award-winning Jazz at Lincoln Center Youth Orchestra.
